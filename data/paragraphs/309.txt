Opening the door to the dining hall, the duke beckons the mentioned knight towards him. Without expecting anything amiss, he enters the room, and the door not only closes behind him but also locks. "Sir Mortimer," the duke solemnly declares, pointing at you, "this noble knight has requested the privilege to duel with you in my presence. Do you trust me to be the judge of your duel?" To refuse would be to deal a mortal insult to Duke Runtu, and that means certain death. Sir Mortimer has no choice but to agree.
