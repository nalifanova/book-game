The crowd falls silent. When the fanfare sounds for the third time, a tall, dark-haired man of about thirty-five years old takes his seat on the throne. A crown with several precious stones adorns his head, and he wears a blue velvet cloak with an embroidered coat of arms—a golden winged lion and next to it a figure of a woman on a green field. "Glory to Duke Runt!" the subjects shout in unison, so you barely have time to open your mouth to avoid standing out too much — 172.
